Our Story

D_Keith, founded in Australia in 2011 by artist KT, began as a restaurant concept aimed at eliminating the need for bins. Since then, the restaurant's owner and chef has transformed it into the world's first zero waste eatery.

The restaurant's mission is to revolutionize the food industry while demonstrating respect for the environment, the food we consume, and our bodies' nourishment. D_Keith achieves this by creating meals using whole ingredients, cutting out unnecessary food processing and transportation, and preserving the food's nutrients and integrity.

The restaurant has its flour mill, which grinds ancient varieties of wheat using traditional methods rather than industrialized bread-making techniques. They make their own butter, oat milk, and oatmeal, and they support a nose-to-tail ideology, ensuring that every part of an animal is used respectfully if it is to be consumed.

D_Keith's furniture and fittings are made from upcycled materials, and the restaurant prioritizes reusing items over recycling them. For example, tables are made from reconstituted food packaging, and light shades are created using mycelium grown on used brewing grains.

To achieve zero waste, the restaurant only accepts deliveries in reusable containers such as crates, pails, urns, or containers. Any waste that remains is compostable, allowing the restaurant to close the loop and create a fully sustainable business model.

D_Keith believes that sustainable food businesses can be financially viable while delivering delicious and natural food. By demonstrating this belief, they aim to encourage the growth of other waste-free businesses through collaboration and by proving that a sustainable model can work.
